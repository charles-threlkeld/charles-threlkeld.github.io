1. Chapter 5 — Computational Models of Speech Acts

With our experimental results in hand, we set about to build models of conversational grammar.

1.1. Models for a Syntax-Derived Schema

In these models, we attempted to apply our results to the syntax-derived taxonomy. We found that our data does not lead to strong improvements on the baselines established by the syntax. We conclude that the taxonomy is not strong enough to provide useful information to the issue.

1.2. Learning a Schema from Conversational Data

In this model, we take a data-first approach, using a low-dimension representation of utterances in order to cluster them in to speech acts. We start with a known set of hand-tagged speech acts, of which we are critical, but believe contain useful information. Using a series of models, estimate the optimal speech act number based on clustering mechanics.

1.3. Robustness Studies

We permuted the data in the above pipeline to establish that it is not fragile with respect to certain key utterances in the corpus. We also applied the same pipeline to another corpus to establish that the clustering is robust to different datasets. There are still limitations to applying this approach to different datasets in different contexts or different taxonomies.
